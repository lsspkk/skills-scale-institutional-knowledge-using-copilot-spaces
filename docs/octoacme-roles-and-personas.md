# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Risk Manager

### Role Summary
Risk Managers identify, assess, monitor, and mitigate project risks throughout the project lifecycle. They ensure that potential threats are proactively managed and communicated to stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and document project risks during planning and execution
- Assess risk impact and likelihood using standardized criteria
- Develop and maintain the Risk Register
- Create and track mitigation plans for high-priority risks
- Facilitate risk review sessions with project teams
- Escalate critical risks to project sponsors and stakeholders
- Collaborate with Project Managers on contingency planning
- Provide risk status updates in stakeholder communications

### Goals
- Minimize project disruptions through proactive risk management
- Ensure risks are transparent and well-communicated
- Build a culture of risk awareness across teams
- Maintain comprehensive risk documentation

### Typical Communication
- Weekly risk reviews with Project Managers
- Monthly risk reports to stakeholders and sponsors
- Ad-hoc communications for newly identified critical risks
- Risk register updates in project status meetings

### Collaboration Points
- **With Project Managers:** Share risk insights, align on mitigation strategies, coordinate escalations
- **With Developers:** Identify technical risks, discuss feasibility of mitigation approaches
- **With Product Managers:** Align on risk priorities relative to business value
- **With Stakeholder Liaison:** Ensure risks are appropriately communicated to external parties
- **With Change Control Coordinator:** Assess risks introduced by proposed changes

---

## Stakeholder Liaison

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Liaisons act as the primary communication bridge between project teams and internal/external stakeholders. They ensure stakeholder needs are captured, feedback is gathered, and project status is effectively communicated.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and map stakeholder groups and their interests
- Develop and maintain stakeholder communication plans
- Facilitate stakeholder feedback sessions and requirements gathering
- Translate stakeholder needs into actionable requirements for the team
- Coordinate stakeholder meetings and presentations
- Ensure timely distribution of project status updates
- Manage stakeholder expectations and address concerns
- Capture and prioritize stakeholder feedback with Product Managers

### Goals
- Maintain strong stakeholder relationships and satisfaction
- Ensure stakeholder voices are heard and acted upon
- Minimize communication gaps and misunderstandings
- Build stakeholder confidence through transparency

### Typical Communication
- Weekly stakeholder status emails or updates
- Monthly stakeholder review meetings
- Regular 1:1 check-ins with key stakeholders
- Feedback summaries shared with project teams

### Collaboration Points
- **With Project Managers:** Coordinate status updates, align on messaging, share stakeholder concerns
- **With Product Managers:** Translate stakeholder feedback into product requirements and priorities
- **With Developers:** Communicate technical updates in stakeholder-friendly language
- **With Risk Manager:** Share stakeholder-related risks and communication needs
- **With Change Control Coordinator:** Communicate changes to affected stakeholders

---

## Change Control Coordinator

### Role Summary
Change Control Coordinators manage the evaluation, approval, documentation, and communication of changes to project scope, requirements, or processes. They ensure changes are properly vetted and recorded for auditability.

### Responsibilities
- Review and triage change requests from team members and stakeholders
- Facilitate Change Control Board meetings and decision-making
- Document all approved and rejected changes with rationale
- Assess impact of proposed changes on timeline, budget, and scope
- Maintain the change log and ensure traceability
- Communicate approved changes to all affected parties
- Work with Project Managers to adjust plans based on approved changes
- Ensure compliance with organizational change management policies

### Goals
- Maintain project scope integrity while allowing necessary flexibility
- Ensure all changes are properly evaluated and documented
- Minimize disruption from uncontrolled changes
- Provide clear audit trail for project decisions

### Typical Communication
- Change request status updates to requestors
- Change Control Board meeting agendas and summaries
- Change impact assessments shared with stakeholders
- Updated change logs distributed to project team

### Collaboration Points
- **With Project Managers:** Assess change impacts on schedule and resources, update project plans
- **With Product Managers:** Evaluate changes against product vision and priorities
- **With Developers:** Understand technical implications of proposed changes
- **With Risk Manager:** Identify risks introduced by changes, incorporate into risk management
- **With Stakeholder Liaison:** Ensure stakeholders are informed of approved changes
