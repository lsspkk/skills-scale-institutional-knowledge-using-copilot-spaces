## Stakeholder Mapping Template

Identify and categorize stakeholders to tailor communication appropriately.

| Stakeholder Name/Group | Role/Department | Interest Level | Influence Level | Communication Preference | Frequency | Primary Contact |
|------------------------|-----------------|----------------|-----------------|--------------------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Example: Executive Sponsor | VP Engineering | High | High | Executive summary email | Monthly | stakeholder@example.com |
| Example: Sales Team | Sales | Medium | Medium | Demo + slides | Milestone-based | sales-lead@example.com |

### Interest & Influence Matrix

- **High Interest + High Influence:** Manage closely (weekly updates, direct engagement)
- **High Interest + Low Influence:** Keep informed (regular updates, open feedback channels)
- **Low Interest + High Influence:** Keep satisfied (periodic briefings, targeted updates)
- **Low Interest + Low Influence:** Monitor (minimal communication, available on request)

## Communication Best Practices

### Clarity
- Use plain language; avoid jargon or explain technical terms
- Be concise but complete
- Use bullet points and headings for scannability

### Consistency
- Send updates on a regular schedule
- Use standard templates for recurring communications
- Maintain consistent tone and format

### Transparency
- Share both good news and challenges
- Provide context for decisions and changes
- Acknowledge uncertainties when they exist

### Actionability
- Clearly state what, if anything, is needed from stakeholders
- Include specific deadlines for decisions or feedback
- Provide contact information for follow-up

### Accessibility
- Store communications in a shared, searchable location
- Accommodate different preferences (email, meetings, dashboards)
- Follow up with stakeholders who don't respond to critical items
